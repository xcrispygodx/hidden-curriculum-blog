322: Skull and Bones and the Tomb That Runs America

18 MIN READ • MARCH 2026 • THE HIDDEN CURRICULUM

In the basement of a windowless brownstone building on the campus of Yale University, the most powerful secret society in America has been initiating new members for nearly two hundred years. The building is known simply as "the Tomb." Its members are known as Bonesmen. And the number they carry — 322 — is stamped on their correspondence, their stationery, and the artifacts within the Tomb itself. What 322 means depends on whom you ask. The society's members do not explain. They do not discuss the society at all. They are forbidden from acknowledging its existence, its rituals, or its membership to anyone who is not a member. The secrecy is absolute, lifelong, and enforced by a bond that transcends political parties, national interests, and public accountability.

Skull and Bones was founded in 1832 by William Huntington Russell and Alphonso Taft at Yale University in New Haven, Connecticut. It is the oldest of Yale's secret societies and, by any objective measure, the most influential. Each year, the society "taps" exactly 15 rising seniors for membership. The tapping occurs in a public ceremony during which Bonesmen walk across campus and physically tap the shoulder of each selected student. The chosen 15 are then inducted into the society through rituals that have never been fully disclosed. They become members for life. Their loyalty to the society supersedes their loyalty to any other institution — including, by the evidence of their careers, the United States government.

◆ The Number 322

The numeral 322 appears on all Skull and Bones correspondence and is displayed prominently within the Tomb. Its meaning has never been officially explained, but two primary theories have persisted for over a century.

The first theory links 322 to the year 322 BCE — the year of the death of Demosthenes, the Athenian statesman and orator who was the last great defender of democratic governance in ancient Greece. Demosthenes spent his career fighting the expansion of Macedonian power under Philip II and Alexander the Great. He was eventually exiled and, upon learning that the Macedonian forces had captured Athens, committed suicide by poisoning. If 322 references Demosthenes, it represents the death of democracy — a symbolic marker that the democratic experiment ended in 322 BCE and that what follows is governance by the elite. Bonesmen, in this interpretation, are the inheritors of the post-democratic order.

The second theory connects 322 to Genesis 3:22 — "And the Lord God said, 'The man has now become like one of us, knowing good and evil. He must not be allowed to reach out his hand and take also from the tree of life and eat, and live forever.'" The verse describes humanity's acquisition of divine knowledge and God's subsequent expulsion of humanity from Eden to prevent immortality. If 322 references Genesis 3:22, it represents the pursuit of forbidden knowledge — the aspiration to become "like one of us," to transcend human limitation through the acquisition of power and understanding that is denied to ordinary people. The symbolism aligns with the society's apparent function: a closed circle of initiated individuals who possess knowledge and connections that the uninitiated do not.

⚠ The Theories of 322

  • 322 BCE: Death of Demosthenes — the last defender of Athenian democracy. Symbolizes the death of democratic governance and the rise of elite rule.
  • Genesis 3:22: "The man has become like one of us, knowing good and evil." Symbolizes the pursuit of forbidden knowledge and the aspiration to divine power.
  • The society's own reference: Skull and Bones identifies itself as the "Chapter 322" of a German student society. The "Chapter 321" was allegedly at a German university, but its existence has never been verified.
  • Dual numbering: The "322" implies a "321" elsewhere — suggesting an international network with a Germanic origin.

A third explanation, offered by some researchers, is that Skull and Bones identifies itself as "Chapter 322" of a German secret society, with "Chapter 321" allegedly located at a German university. William Huntington Russell studied in Germany before founding the society and was reportedly influenced by German student dueling societies and their ritualistic traditions. If this is the origin, the number implies an international network — a transatlantic brotherhood with chapters in both the United States and Europe. The German connection has never been conclusively verified, but the society's early rituals incorporated German-language elements, and the influence of German academic secret societies on Yale's campus culture in the 1830s is documented.

◆ The Tomb

The Skull and Bones meeting hall — universally referred to as "the Tomb" — is a windowless, sandstone building on High Street in the center of Yale's campus. It was built in 1856 and expanded in 1903 and 1911. The building has no windows on its ground floor. The entrance is a heavy wooden door with iron fixtures. The interior has never been photographed with the society's permission and has been described only in fragmentary accounts by former members who chose, often decades later, to speak.

According to these accounts, the Tomb's interior includes a room called the "Parlor" or "Reception Room," decorated with artwork and artifacts accumulated over nearly two centuries. The "Tomb Room" or "Inner Temple" is the initiation chamber, where the society's rituals are conducted. It reportedly contains human skulls, bones, and other skeletal artifacts. The "Room 322" is the society's private sanctum. A grandfather clock in the Tomb is reportedly stopped at a specific time. The interior decor is said to include Nazi memorabilia, including a set of Hitler's silverware, allegedly acquired during or after World War II. The presence of such artifacts in a building on an American university campus, maintained by men who have held the highest offices in the United States government, is either darkly ironic or genuinely disturbing, depending on one's interpretation of the society's purpose.

Two skulls are reportedly displayed in the Tomb. One is identified as belonging to "Geronimo" — the Apache war leader who died in 1909 at Fort Sill, Oklahoma. The claim that Bonesmen stole Geronimo's remains from his grave at Fort Sill in 1918 has been the subject of a lawsuit filed by Geronimo's descendants against Skull and Bones, Yale University, and the federal government. The lawsuit, Geronimo v. Obama (later amended), alleged that Prescott Bush — grandfather of George W. Bush and a Skull and Bones member — participated in the grave robbery along with other Bonesmen stationed at Fort Sill during World War I. The case was dismissed on jurisdictional grounds. The society has never officially confirmed or denied the presence of Geronimo's remains.

◆ The Bonesmen: Presidents and Power

The alumni roster of Skull and Bones is not a list of marginal figures. It is a directory of American institutional power spanning politics, intelligence, finance, law, and academia.

George Herbert Walker Bush was tapped in 1948. He served as Director of Central Intelligence (1976–1977), Vice President (1981–1989), and President of the United States (1989–1993). As CIA Director, he oversaw the intelligence community during a period of significant covert operations. As President, he directed the Gulf War — Operation Desert Storm — which established the permanent American military presence in the Middle East that continues to this day. His father, Prescott Bush, was also a Bonesman, a US Senator, and a partner at Brown Brothers Harriman — the investment bank that was, during the 1930s and 1940s, the main Wall Street connection for German industrial firms with direct ties to the Nazi regime.

George Walker Bush was tapped in 1968. He served as Governor of Texas (1995–2000) and President of the United States (2001–2009). His presidency was defined by the September 11 attacks, the invasion of Afghanistan, the invasion of Iraq, the creation of the Department of Homeland Security, the Patriot Act, and the expansion of executive surveillance powers. The wars he initiated generated trillions of dollars in defense spending and reshaped the geopolitical structure of the Middle East. His father and grandfather were both Bonesmen.

John Forbes Kerry was tapped in 1966. He served as a US Senator from Massachusetts (1985–2013) and as Secretary of State under President Obama (2013–2017). In the 2004 presidential election, Kerry ran against George W. Bush. The election was two members of the same secret society — Skull and Bones — running against each other for the presidency of the United States. Neither candidate mentioned this during the campaign. When asked about Skull and Bones during a debate, both men laughed and declined to discuss it. The media did not pursue the question. The American public was asked to choose between two initiates of the same secret brotherhood, and the fact was presented as a curiosity rather than a structural reality.

◆ The Intelligence Connection

The connection between Skull and Bones and the Central Intelligence Agency is not incidental. George H.W. Bush served as CIA Director. But the relationship extends far beyond a single individual. The CIA's early leadership was drawn heavily from Yale's class of the 1940s and 1950s — a class heavily influenced by Skull and Bones and its sister societies. The agency's culture of secrecy, compartmentalization, and institutional loyalty mirrors the culture of the secret society from which many of its officers were recruited.

W. Averell Harriman, a Bonesman, served as Ambassador to the Soviet Union during World War II, as Secretary of Commerce under Truman, and as Governor of New York. His brother, E. Roland Harriman, was also a Bonesman and a partner at Brown Brothers Harriman. The Harriman family's financial empire was deeply intertwined with both the intelligence community and the political establishment. McGeorge Bundy, National Security Advisor to both Kennedy and Johnson, was a Bonesman who played a central role in escalating the Vietnam War — a conflict that generated enormous defense spending and served the strategic interests of the military-industrial complex that Bonesmen helped build.

Henry Stimson, Secretary of War during World War II, was a Bonesman who oversaw the Manhattan Project — the classified program that built the atomic bomb. The connection between Bonesmen and the most consequential military and intelligence decisions of the 20th century is not a coincidence. It is a pattern. The society selects individuals who are destined for institutional power, provides them with a lifelong network of mutual loyalty, and places them in positions where their decisions shape national policy. The society does not direct their decisions. It does not need to. The shared culture, the shared loyalties, and the shared understanding of how power operates ensure that Bonesmen will act in ways that maintain and extend the system that elevated them.

◆ The 2004 Election: Two Bonesmen

The 2004 presidential election between George W. Bush and John Kerry was, in a structural sense, the most revealing election in modern American history. Two members of the same secret society, initiated through the same rituals, bound by the same lifelong oaths of secrecy, competed for the most powerful office in the world. The election was presented to the public as a contest between fundamentally different visions for America — conservative vs. liberal, war vs. nuanced war, tax cuts vs. different tax cuts.

The structural reality was different. Both candidates were members of a society that prioritizes institutional loyalty over public accountability. Both candidates were products of the same elite pipeline that moves from Yale to the corridors of power. Both candidates refused to discuss Skull and Bones when asked, with a coordination that suggested a shared understanding that the society's existence was, at minimum, an awkward topic. The media treated the shared membership as a trivial oddity. It was not discussed in any substantive way during the campaign. The American public was never told what it means that both candidates for president were members of the same secret society.

Whether Skull and Bones actively coordinates political outcomes is unknown and, given the society's impenetrable secrecy, probably unknowable. What is known is that the society has placed members in the presidency, the vice presidency, the CIA directorship, multiple cabinet positions, and congressional leadership for generations. The probability that a single secret society with approximately 15 new members per year would produce this density of political leadership by chance alone is vanishingly small. The society is either extraordinarily lucky in its selections or extraordinarily effective in its influence. Occam's Razor favors the latter.

◆ The Broader Network

Skull and Bones is not the only elite secret society at Yale — Scroll and Key and Wolf's Head are its primary competitors — nor is it the only node in the network of elite coordination. But it is the most visible and the most extensively documented, thanks largely to the political careers of its most prominent members. The society intersects with the Bohemian Grove, where Bonesmen and other elite club members gather annually. It connects to the Bilderberg Group, where American and European elites coordinate policy. It feeds into the defense-intelligence establishment, where classified programs are funded by budgets that have never been audited. And it operates within the petrodollar architecture, where the financial interests of the American elite and the military interests of the American state are structurally inseparable.

The Tomb has no windows because what happens inside is not meant to be seen. The members have no public loyalties because their true loyalty was sealed in a basement ceremony before they ever took an oath of office. And the number 322 marks them as members of a brotherhood that has survived two centuries of American democracy — not by participating in it, but by quietly controlling the institutions that pretend it exists. — On the Architecture of Hidden Loyalty

The significance of Skull and Bones is not in its rituals, its symbols, or its occult paraphernalia. It is in its function. The society is a mechanism for identifying, cultivating, and binding the individuals who will control American institutional power. It operates outside democratic oversight, outside public scrutiny, and outside the accountability structures that are supposed to constrain the exercise of power in a republic. It is a parallel institution that shapes the public institution from within. And it has been doing so, without interruption, since 1832.
