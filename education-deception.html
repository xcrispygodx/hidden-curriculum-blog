The Prussian Model: Manufacturing Obedience at Scale

In the early 1800s, Prussia developed a compulsory education system with one explicit goal: produce citizens who would obey authority without question. Soldiers, factory workers, bureaucrats — the system was designed for compliance, not critical thinking. After Prussia's military victories, American industrialists imported this model wholesale. Horace Mann traveled to Prussia in 1843 and brought back the blueprint that would become American public education. The timing was not coincidental — the Industrial Revolution needed workers who would follow orders, not question them.
