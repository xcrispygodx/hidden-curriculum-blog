The Owl in the Woods:
Bohemian Grove and the Cremation of Care

18 MIN READ • MARCH 2026 • THE HIDDEN CURRICULUM

Every July, in a redwood forest in Monte Rio, California, approximately 2,500 of the most powerful men in the world gather for a two-week retreat. They arrive as presidents, Fortune 500 CEOs, military commanders, intelligence directors, media moguls, and members of the political elite. They leave their public identities at the gate. No press is admitted. No recordings are made. No minutes are published. What happens inside the Bohemian Grove is protected by a wall of silence that has held, with very few exceptions, for over 150 years.

The Bohemian Club was founded in 1872 in San Francisco as a private club for artists, musicians, writers, and wealthy patrons. Its membership evolved over the decades from the cultural elite to the political and financial elite. The club's motto is "Weaving spiders come not here" — a line from Shakespeare's A Midsummer Night's Dream meaning that business dealings are discouraged at the retreat. The motto is, by all available evidence, a fiction. Business is the point. Power is the currency. The retreat is where the deals that shape nations are made in the spaces between the theatrical performances and the alcohol.

◆ The Grove: Geography and Structure

The Bohemian Grove is a 2,700-acre private campground situated along the Russian River in Sonoma County, California. The property is dense with old-growth redwood trees — some over a thousand years old — creating a natural cathedral of towering columns and filtered light. The grove is divided into approximately 118 camps, each with its own lodge, dining facilities, and sleeping accommodations. Some camps are modest cabins. Others are elaborate compounds with multiple buildings, private chefs, and dedicated staff. The camps have names like Mandalay, Cave Man, Hillbillies, Owl's Nest, and Lost Angels. Each has its own character, its own membership hierarchy, and its own traditions.

The grove contains several notable structures and locations. The Grove Stage is an outdoor amphitheater used for the club's theatrical productions — elaborate plays, musicals, and variety shows performed by members. The Field Circle is an open-air venue used for talks, lectures, and musical performances. The Lake is a small artificial body of water at the center of the grove, used for swimming, boating, and as the setting for the Cremation of Care ceremony. And presiding over it all, at the head of the lake, is the Owl Shrine — a 40-foot concrete owl that serves as the spiritual centerpiece of the grove.

⚠ Bohemian Grove by the Numbers

  • Founded: 1872 (Bohemian Club); first summer encampment held shortly after.
  • Location: Monte Rio, Sonoma County, California.
  • Size: 2,700 acres of old-growth redwood forest.
  • Duration: Two weeks each July.
  • Attendees: Approximately 2,500 per year (membership estimated at ~2,500 — some attend annually, others intermittently).
  • Camps: ~118 individual camps within the grove.
  • Owl Shrine: 40-foot concrete owl at the head of the lake.
  • Secrecy: No media permitted. No recording devices. Strict non-disclosure by members.

The club's membership has included every Republican president since Calvin Coolidge (and some Democrats), multiple CIA directors, defense secretaries, Joint Chiefs of Staff members, Supreme Court justices, senators, governors, and the CEOs of major corporations including those in oil, banking, defense contracting, and media. The membership list is not officially published, but partial lists have been compiled from public records, leaked documents, and the rare investigative journalism that has penetrated the grove's perimeter.

◆ The Cremation of Care

The Cremation of Care is the opening ceremony of the Bohemian Grove's summer encampment. It is performed on the first Saturday of the retreat, at dusk, at the lake in the center of the grove. The ceremony involves a theatrical production in which a figure representing "Dull Care" — the cares, worries, and burdens of the outside world — is symbolically burned in effigy at the foot of the Owl Shrine. The production features robed figures, chanting, dramatic lighting, and a boat that carries the effigy across the lake to the owl, where it is set ablaze. The message is clear: the attendees are here to shed the burdens of their public lives. What follows is their own.

The ceremony's theatrical elements have been described by attendees and by the rare outsiders who have witnessed or documented it. The high priest of the ceremony delivers an address to the owl, asking it to accept the sacrifice of Dull Care so that the members may be free. The owl responds — a booming, amplified voice emanating from the concrete statue. The effigy is burned. The participants cheer. The retreat begins.

The owl is traditionally associated with Athena, the Greek goddess of wisdom, and with Minerva, her Roman counterpart. The Bohemian Club's logo features an owl, and the club's symbolism is drawn heavily from classical references. But the owl also carries associations with Moloch — the ancient deity to whom children were reportedly sacrificed in the Hebrew Bible and in historical accounts of Carthaginian and Phoenician religious practice. The association between owls and Moloch is not universal, and the Bohemian Club's use of the owl is officially derived from its classical wisdom symbolism. But the practice of burning an effigy at the foot of a giant owl statue, in a ceremony attended by men who control nuclear arsenals and trillion-dollar budgets, invites interpretation beyond the classical.

The president of the United States, the director of the CIA, and the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff are gathered in a forest at night, in robes, chanting at a 40-foot owl, burning an effigy of their worries, and then spending two weeks together in private. This is not a conspiracy theory. This is a documented annual event. — On the Normalization of Ritual

◆ Presidential Attendance

The Bohemian Grove's guest lists, compiled from various sources over the decades, read like a directory of American power. Every Republican president from Calvin Coolidge through George W. Bush has been a member of the Bohemian Club. Herbert Hoover was a lifelong member and attended the grove regularly. Dwight D. Eisenhower attended as president. Richard Nixon attended before and during his presidency. Ronald Reagan attended while governor of California and as president. George H.W. Bush attended regularly. George W. Bush attended as governor of Texas.

Richard Nixon's private comments about the grove, captured on his White House taping system, are revealing. He called it "the most faggy goddamn thing you could ever imagine" — a crude but unambiguous expression of his assessment of the grove's rituals and atmosphere. Nixon attended but was clearly uncomfortable with the ceremonial aspects. His description confirms that the Cremation of Care and other rituals were, at minimum, theatrical spectacles that even a sitting president found bizarre.

Democratic presidents have been less prominently associated with the grove, but the club's membership has included prominent Democrats. The bipartisan nature of the membership is significant: the grove transcends party politics. Republicans and Democrats who disagree publicly on every policy question socialize together in private at the grove. The implication is that the political disagreements are performative — that the real alignment of interests is among the elite regardless of party, and that the grove is one of the spaces where that alignment is maintained.

◆ The Manhattan Project Connection

One of the most significant historical claims about the Bohemian Grove is that the Manhattan Project — the classified program that developed the atomic bomb during World War II — was discussed and planned, at least in part, at the grove. The claim is supported by the known attendance of key Manhattan Project figures at the grove and by the known use of private elite gatherings as venues for discussing classified programs that could not be addressed in official government settings.

Ernest Lawrence, the physicist who invented the cyclotron and played a central role in the Manhattan Project's uranium enrichment program, was a member of the Bohemian Club. So was Ernest O. Lawrence's close associate, the physicist Robert Oppenheimer, who directed the Los Alamos laboratory where the bomb was designed. Other Manhattan Project participants who were Bohemian Club members or guests have been identified in membership records and historical accounts. The grove provided a private, secure, and socially exclusive environment in which the most sensitive military project in history could be discussed outside the constraints of official government channels.

The significance of this connection is not merely historical. It establishes a pattern: the Bohemian Grove is a venue where classified, controversial, and consequential decisions are discussed by the people who have the power to make them, outside any framework of democratic accountability. The Manhattan Project — a program that produced a weapon capable of destroying cities and that reshaped the entire geopolitical structure of the world — was planned, at least in part, in a private club in a redwood forest. The precedent has never been repudiated.

◆ Media, Secrecy, and Alex Jones

The Bohemian Grove's secrecy is enforced through multiple mechanisms. The club's bylaws prohibit members from discussing grove activities with non-members. The physical perimeter of the 2,700-acre property is patrolled by private security and, during encampments, by Sonoma County Sheriff's deputies. The grove is located in a remote area accessible by a single road, making surveillance of arrivals and departures feasible. Media access has been denied consistently for the club's entire history.

The most famous infiltration of the Bohemian Grove was conducted by conspiracy researcher Alex Jones in July 2000. Jones, accompanied by a cameraman, entered the grove through the surrounding forest, avoiding security patrols. He filmed portions of the Cremation of Care ceremony and captured footage of the owl shrine, the burning effigy, and the robed participants. The footage was released as the documentary Dark Secrets: Inside Bohemian Grove and became one of the most widely viewed pieces of conspiracy media in the pre-YouTube era. Jones's footage confirmed the basic elements of the Cremation of Care that had been described by previous writers and researchers but never filmed.

Earlier investigative work was conducted by journalist Philip Weiss, who infiltrated the grove in 1989 for Spy magazine. Weiss's account, published under the title "Masters of the Universe Go to Camp," described the grove's atmosphere as a combination of fraternity party, summer camp, and elite networking event. He confirmed the Cremation of Care, the owl shrine, the theatrical productions, and the pervasive culture of alcohol and male bonding. Weiss's account was less sensational than Jones's footage but equally revealing in its depiction of the world's most powerful men behaving, in private, with a casualness that their public personas never permit.

◆ The Owl, Moloch, and Symbolic Interpretation

The symbolism of the Bohemian Grove resists simple interpretation. The owl, in classical Western tradition, represents wisdom, knowledge, and the watchfulness of Athena/Minerva. The Bohemian Club's adoption of the owl is officially grounded in this tradition. The Cremation of Care is officially a theatrical ritual representing the shedding of worldly concerns — an artistic performance, not a religious ceremony.

But symbols operate on multiple levels simultaneously. The owl is also associated with nocturnal predation, with hidden sight, with the ability to see in darkness. The burning of an effigy at the foot of a giant owl, performed by men who control military, financial, and political systems, carries an implication that transcends the classical. Whether the participants understand the ritual as symbolic theater or as something more literal, the effect is the same: it creates a shared experience of initiation, secrecy, and group identity among the world's most powerful men. It bonds them through a shared act that they cannot discuss publicly. It creates an in-group whose shared secret is, itself, a form of power.

The association with Moloch — the ancient deity of child sacrifice — is the most inflammatory interpretation and the one most frequently dismissed by mainstream commentators. The historical Moloch was a deity of Ammonite and Carthaginian origin, associated in the Hebrew Bible with the sacrifice of children by fire. The Bohemian Grove's burning effigy, performed in the presence of a giant owl that some interpret as Moloch, is the basis for this association. The interpretation may be overwrought. But the question of why the world's most powerful men chose as their central ritual the burning of an effigy at the foot of a giant nocturnal predator is not answered by the claim that it is "just theater." All rituals are "just theater" to those who do not share their meaning. To those who do, they are the architecture of identity.

◆ The Network of Influence

The Bohemian Grove does not exist in isolation. It is one node in a network of elite organizations that span the same membership, the same institutional connections, and the same pattern of private coordination outside democratic accountability. The Skull and Bones society at Yale taps 15 members per year for life initiation. The Bilderberg Group convenes 130 attendees annually under Chatham House Rules. The defense-intelligence establishment rotates officials between government, industry, and private-sector positions through the revolving door. The petrodollar architecture ensures that the financial interests of the elite and the military interests of the state are structurally aligned.

The Bohemian Grove is not the cause of this system. It is a symptom — and a mechanism. It provides the physical space, the social ritual, and the interpersonal bonding that maintain the elite's cohesion across decades, across administrations, and across the public political divisions that divide everyone else. The Democrat and the Republican who argue on television about tax policy on Monday will share a camp at the Bohemian Grove on Tuesday. The generals who testify before Congress about military readiness will sit beside the defense contractors who profit from that readiness, in a forest where no one can see what they discuss. The system works because the people who control it know each other, trust each other, and coordinate privately. The grove is where that happens.

The owl watches in the dark because it can see what others cannot. The men who gather at its feet do the same. — On the Function of Private Rituals in Public Power

The Bohemian Grove will continue to meet every July. The Cremation of Care will continue to be performed. The owl will continue to preside. And the men who gather there will continue to shape the world from inside a forest that the public is forbidden to enter. The secret is not what happens at the grove. The secret is that it is considered normal.
