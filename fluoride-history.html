The Waste They Put in Your Water:
Fluoride's Industrial Origins

16 MIN READ • MARCH 2026 • THE HIDDEN CURRICULUM

You drink it every day. You bathe in it. You cook with it. It is in your toothpaste, your mouthwash, and the water that flows from every tap in your home. Fluoride — the ionic form of the element fluorine — is added to the drinking water of approximately 73% of the American population, over 200 million people, under the premise that it prevents dental cavities. The Centers for Disease Control called water fluoridation one of the ten great public health achievements of the 20th century. The American Dental Association endorses it. The World Health Organization supports it. The consensus, we are told, is settled.

But the story of how fluoride entered your water supply is not a story of medical breakthrough. It is a story of industrial waste disposal, legal liability, and a public relations campaign so effective that it transformed a toxic byproduct of aluminum and phosphate manufacturing into a medicine that the government puts in your drinking water without your individual consent. The connection between fluoride and dental health was not discovered by dentists. It was promoted by lawyers, defended by the same scientists who defended leaded gasoline, and funded by the industries that needed somewhere to put their most dangerous waste.

◆ The Aluminum Industry Connection

The modern story of water fluoridation begins not in a dental office but in an aluminum smelting plant. The Aluminum Company of America — ALCOA — was founded in 1888 by Charles Martin Hall and grew into one of the largest industrial corporations in the world. Aluminum smelting, the process of extracting aluminum from bauxite ore using the Hall-Héroult electrolytic process, produces massive quantities of fluoride waste. Sodium fluoride, hydrofluorosilicic acid, and silicon tetrafluoride are all byproducts of aluminum production. In the 1930s and 1940s, these waste products were piling up. They were toxic. They were corrosive. They were contaminating water supplies near ALCOA plants, and ALCOA was facing lawsuits from farmers whose livestock was being poisoned by airborne fluoride emissions.

The company needed a solution. The solution was not to stop producing fluoride waste — that would have meant stopping aluminum production. The solution was to reclassify the waste. If fluoride could be sold as a health product rather than disposed of as industrial waste, the problem would be eliminated. Not only would the liability disappear, but the waste would become a revenue stream. This is not speculation. This is the documented business logic that drove the fluoridation campaign from its inception.

⚠ The ALCOA Pipeline

  • ALCOA produced massive fluoride waste from aluminum smelting — sodium fluoride, hydrofluorosilicic acid, silicon tetrafluoride.
  • Fluoride emissions from ALCOA plants poisoned livestock and contaminated waterways near smelting facilities, generating lawsuits.
  • Oscar Ewing — ALCOA's lead attorney — was appointed head of the US Public Health Service in 1947.
  • Within two years of Ewing's appointment, the US Public Health Service endorsed water fluoridation.
  • The fluoride used in municipal water systems is not pharmaceutical-grade sodium fluoride — it is hydrofluorosilicic acid, recovered from phosphate fertilizer manufacturing and aluminum smelting waste.
  • This "water treatment chemical" is classified by the EPA as hazardous waste when not added to drinking water.

Oscar Ewing was ALCOA's top lawyer. In 1947, he was appointed by President Truman to head the Federal Security Agency, which oversaw the US Public Health Service. Within two years, the Public Health Service — the same agency Ewing now controlled — officially endorsed water fluoridation as a public health measure. The timing is not subtle. The conflict of interest is not hidden. It is simply not discussed. Ewing's appointment placed the aluminum industry's legal representative in charge of the federal agency responsible for determining whether fluoride was safe to add to public water. He determined that it was. The aluminum industry's waste problem was solved.

◆ The Manhattan Project Connection

The fluoride story intersects with another classified program that defined the 20th century: the Manhattan Project. Fluorine and fluoride compounds were essential to the uranium enrichment process that produced the fissile material for the atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki. The gaseous diffusion process used to separate uranium-235 from uranium-238 required uranium hexafluoride (UF6), a highly corrosive and toxic fluoride compound. The Manhattan Project's production facilities at Oak Ridge, Tennessee, and Paducah, Kentucky, consumed enormous quantities of fluoride and generated enormous quantities of fluoride waste.

By the late 1940s, fluoride waste from the Manhattan Project and its successor, the Atomic Energy Commission, was a serious liability problem. Communities near the production facilities were experiencing fluoride-related health effects — dental fluorosis, skeletal fluorosis, respiratory damage — and were beginning to ask questions. The Manhattan Project's own medical research division, led by Dr. Harold Hodge, conducted classified studies on the toxicity of fluoride. These studies confirmed that fluoride was harmful at concentrations well below those being proposed for water fluoridation. The results were classified.

Harold Hodge occupied a remarkable dual role. As chief toxicologist of the Manhattan Project, he oversaw classified research documenting fluoride's toxicity to humans. As a leading advocate for water fluoridation, he publicly testified that fluoride was safe. He held both positions simultaneously. The classified research he conducted as a weapons scientist contradicted the public assurances he gave as a public health advocate. This is not a conspiracy theory. It is documented in declassified Manhattan Project records.

The connection between the Manhattan Project's fluoride waste problem and the water fluoridation campaign is not coincidental. Both the aluminum industry and the nuclear weapons program needed to dispose of fluoride waste. Both industries benefited from a public narrative that fluoride was not a toxin but a medicine. And both industries had the financial resources and political connections to shape that narrative. The convergence of industrial waste disposal needs and public health policy is the central, unexamined fact of the fluoridation story.

◆ Robert Kehoe and the Kettering Laboratory

The scientific defense of fluoride was conducted by the same institution and, in some cases, the same individuals who had defended the most infamous industrial toxin of the 20th century: leaded gasoline. Robert Kehoe was the director of the Kettering Laboratory at the University of Cincinnati — a research institution funded by the Ethyl Corporation, General Motors, and DuPont. Kehoe spent decades producing research that minimized the health risks of tetraethyl lead, the additive that made gasoline profitable and caused neurological damage to hundreds of millions of people worldwide. His research methodology was designed to produce the conclusions his funders required.

Kehoe applied the same methodology to fluoride. Under contract with ALCOA and other industrial clients, the Kettering Laboratory produced studies concluding that fluoride was safe at the concentrations proposed for water fluoridation. The studies were funded by the industries that produced fluoride waste. The conclusions were foregone. This is the same Robert Kehoe who testified before Congress that leaded gasoline was safe — testimony that delayed the removal of lead from gasoline for decades, during which time an entire generation of children suffered irreversible neurological damage from lead exposure.

The Kettering Laboratory's role in both the leaded gasoline and fluoride debates is not a footnote. It is a pattern. The same institutional structure that industrialized lead poisoning also industrialized fluoride promotion. The same funding model — industry money producing industry-favorable research — operated in both cases. The same dismissal of independent researchers who found evidence of harm was applied in both cases. Leaded gasoline was eventually banned, decades after the evidence of harm was conclusive. Fluoride remains in the water supply.

◆ The Phyllis Mullenix Case

In 1995, Dr. Phyllis Mullenix published a peer-reviewed study in the journal Neurotoxicology and Teratology demonstrating that fluoride exposure in rats caused changes in brain chemistry and behavior consistent with neurological damage. Her research, conducted at the Forsyth Dental Center in Boston — a prestigious institution affiliated with Harvard — found that fluoride accumulated in the brain and affected the central nervous system, particularly in developing organisms. The implications for human children, whose developing brains are uniquely vulnerable to neurotoxic substances, were significant.

Mullenix was fired from the Forsyth Dental Center within weeks of her paper's publication. Her laboratory was sealed. Her research materials were confiscated. Her career in American academic research was effectively terminated. Forsyth's director told her that her research had made it impossible for the institution to continue receiving funding. The funding sources were not specified, but Forsyth received grants from the National Institute of Dental and Craniofacial Research — an institute with a long institutional commitment to the fluoride narrative.

When a scientist is fired for publishing peer-reviewed research, the question is not whether the science is wrong. The question is who benefits from it being suppressed. — On the Termination of Phyllis Mullenix

Mullenix's findings were not an isolated anomaly. In 2012, a Harvard School of Public Health meta-analysis published in Environmental Health Perspectives reviewed 27 studies, primarily from China, examining the relationship between fluoride exposure and children's intelligence. The meta-analysis found a strong, consistent association: children in high-fluoride areas had significantly lower IQ scores than children in low-fluoride areas. The average IQ difference was 7 points — a substantial deficit at the population level. The Harvard researchers noted that the fluoride concentrations in many of the Chinese studies were higher than those used in American water fluoridation, but concluded that the results "support the possibility of an adverse effect of high fluoride exposure on children's neurodevelopment."

Subsequent research has reinforced these findings. A 2017 study funded by the National Institutes of Health and published in Environmental Health Perspectives followed 299 mother-child pairs in Mexico and found that higher prenatal fluoride exposure was associated with lower IQ scores in children at ages 4 and 6-12. A 2019 Canadian study published in JAMA Pediatrics examined 512 mother-child pairs and found that maternal fluoride exposure during pregnancy was associated with lower IQ scores in boys. The evidence is not conclusive in the way that a randomized controlled trial would be — ethical constraints prevent giving pregnant women fluoride to test whether their children's IQ decreases. But the consistency of the association across multiple populations, methodologies, and research teams is difficult to dismiss.

◆ Fluoride in Pharmaceuticals and Chemical Weapons

The presence of fluorine in pharmaceutical compounds and chemical weapons is not coincidental. Fluorine is the most electronegative element — the most aggressively reactive nonmetal on the periodic table. It forms extraordinarily strong bonds with carbon, which is why fluorinated compounds are used as pesticides (fipronil), anesthetics (sevoflurane, desflurane), anti-inflammatory drugs (dexamethasone), and psychotropic medications. The carbon-fluorine bond is so strong that fluorinated compounds resist metabolic breakdown in the body, which is both their pharmaceutical utility and their environmental persistence.

Fluoxetine — the active ingredient in Prozac, the most widely prescribed antidepressant in history — is a fluorinated compound. The fluorine atom in fluoxetine is not incidental to its pharmacological activity. It increases the drug's lipophilicity, allowing it to cross the blood-brain barrier more effectively, and it resists metabolic degradation, extending the drug's half-life in the body. The same chemical property that makes fluoride persistent in the environment makes fluorinated drugs persistent in the human body.

Sarin nerve gas — the organophosphate compound used in the 1995 Tokyo subway attack and in multiple chemical weapons attacks in Syria — contains a phosphorus-fluorine bond. The fluoride ion is the component of sarin that inhibits acetylcholinesterase, the enzyme that breaks down the neurotransmitter acetylcholine. Without this enzyme, acetylcholine accumulates at nerve synapses, causing continuous nerve firing, muscle paralysis, respiratory failure, and death. The mechanism of nerve agent poisoning is fluoride-mediated. This does not mean that drinking fluoridated water is equivalent to sarin exposure — the concentrations are orders of magnitude different. But it does mean that the statement "fluoride is safe" requires qualification. The dose makes the poison. And the dose that is safe has not been established with the rigor that the public health establishment claims.

◆ The European Rejection

Approximately 97% of Western Europe does not fluoridate its drinking water. This is not because European nations lack access to the technology or because their public health systems are less advanced than America's. It is because European health authorities have evaluated the evidence and concluded that water fluoridation is either unnecessary, insufficiently supported, or ethically unacceptable. The countries that do not fluoridate include Germany, France, the Netherlands, Belgium, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, Finland, Austria, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, and Japan.

The dental health outcomes in non-fluoridating European nations are not worse than those in fluoridating countries. Tooth decay rates have declined steadily across all developed nations over the past 50 years — in fluoridating and non-fluoridating countries alike. The decline is attributed to improved nutrition, better dental hygiene, and the widespread availability of fluoride toothpaste, which delivers fluoride directly to the teeth without requiring systemic ingestion. The argument that water fluoridation is necessary for dental health is contradicted by the experience of nations that do not fluoridate and have equal or better dental outcomes.

Several European countries that once fluoridated their water have stopped. The Netherlands discontinued fluoridation in 1976 after its Supreme Court ruled that medication of public water supplies without individual consent violated the constitutional right to bodily integrity. Germany never fluoridated, based on the principle that public water is not a vehicle for medication. Finland fluoridated from 1959 to 1993, then discontinued after public opposition and a review of evidence suggesting that the risks outweighed the benefits. These decisions were made by public health authorities with access to the same scientific literature as American agencies. They reached different conclusions. The divergence suggests that the "scientific consensus" on fluoridation is not as universal as its advocates claim.

◆ The Phosphate Fertilizer Connection

The hydrofluorosilicic acid added to most municipal water systems in the United States is not pharmaceutical-grade sodium fluoride. It is a byproduct of the phosphate fertilizer industry. When phosphate rock is processed to produce phosphoric acid — the primary ingredient in agricultural fertilizers — the process generates silicon tetrafluoride gas, which is captured in wet scrubbers and sold as hydrofluorosilicic acid. This substance is classified as hazardous waste by the EPA when it is collected from the scrubber systems. It becomes a "water treatment chemical" when it is sold to municipal water departments. The classification changes. The substance does not.

The phosphate fertilizer industry — dominated by companies like Mosaic, Nutrien, and CF Industries — generates millions of gallons of hydrofluorosilicic acid annually. Disposing of this material as hazardous waste is expensive. Selling it to water treatment facilities as a fluoridation chemical generates revenue. The economic incentive to promote water fluoridation is not subtle. The industry that produces fluoride waste is the same industry that sells fluoride for water fluoridation. The waste product is the profit product. And the cities that buy it are not required to disclose its industrial origin to their residents.

This is not a footnote in the fluoridation story. It is the economic engine. Without the phosphate fertilizer and aluminum industries' need to dispose of fluoride waste — profitably — the water fluoridation campaign would never have had the financial backing to achieve the saturation it has in the American water supply. The science was secondary. The waste disposal was primary. And the public was never told.

The substance added to your drinking water is classified as hazardous waste by the EPA in its original form. It becomes a "treatment chemical" only when it enters the municipal water system. The chemistry does not change. The classification does. And no one asks why. — On the Dual Classification of Hydrofluorosilicic Acid

◆ The Broader Pattern

The fluoride story is not isolated. It fits a pattern that appears across the petroleum industry, the energy sector, and the pharmaceutical establishment: industrial waste is reclassified as a product, adverse health effects are suppressed by industry-funded research, and public policy is shaped by the economic interests of the industries that benefit. The petroleum industry turned oil waste into plastics and saturated the planet with microplastics. The lead industry turned tetraethyl lead into a gasoline additive and poisoned an entire generation. The fluoride industry turned smelting waste into a "medicine" and medicated 200 million Americans without consent.

The common thread is not conspiracy. It is capitalism operating in a regulatory environment that it controls. When the industries that produce toxic waste also fund the research that determines whether that waste is toxic, the outcome is predetermined. When the lawyers for those industries are appointed to run the federal agencies responsible for regulating them, the regulation is predetermined. When the scientists who find evidence of harm are fired and the studies that find evidence of safety are published, the scientific consensus is manufactured, not discovered.

Fluoride in your water is not the most dangerous thing in your environment. It may not even be dangerous at the concentrations used in water fluoridation — though the growing body of evidence on neurodevelopmental effects suggests that this question is far from settled. The danger is the system that put it there: an industrial waste disposal pipeline disguised as a public health measure, defended by industry-funded science, implemented by captured regulators, and maintained by a public that was never told the full story.
